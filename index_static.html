About

I'm an undergraduate in Mathematics at UC Berkeley (GPA 4.00). My interests span algebraic topology (degree theory, topological obstructions), stochastic modeling for complex systems, and computational biology (computer vision and morphometrics for pollinators). Previously, I studied at Vanderbilt University and UC Santa Barbara.

I enjoy projects that connect rigorous theory with computation—ranging from degree-theoretic pathways to the Hairy Ball Theorem to image-based analysis of bee patterning and 3D morphology.
